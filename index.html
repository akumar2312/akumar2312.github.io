Research Bio

I am a computational geophysicist with expertise in integrating machine learning and numerical modeling to address complex geoscientific and environmental problems. I earned my Ph.D. through a joint doctoral program between IIT Bombay and Monash University.

My research spans geophysics, hydrology, and applied artificial intelligence, with emphasis on inverse problems, reduced-order modeling, surrogate modeling, and operational flood forecasting.

Research Interests

  • Geophysical inversion and Bayesian analysis
  • Reduced-order and surrogate modeling
  • AI/ML applications in geoscience and hydrology
  • Urban flood forecasting and decision-support systems
  • High-performance numerical modeling

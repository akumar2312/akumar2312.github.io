Research Bio

I am a computational geophysicist with expertise in integrating machine learning and numerical modeling to address complex geoscientific and environmental problems. I earned my Ph.D. through a joint doctoral program between the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay and Monash University, Australia.

My research spans geophysics, hydrology, and applied artificial intelligence, with particular emphasis on inverse problems, reduced-order modeling, surrogate modeling, and operational flood forecasting. I currently serve as Principal Project Scientist at IIT Delhi, where I lead the development of reduced-order and surrogate models for real-time urban flood prediction in support of disaster risk reduction.

Research Interests

  • Geophysical inversion and Bayesian analysis
  • Reduced-order and surrogate modeling
  • AI/ML applications in geoscience and hydrology
  • Urban flood forecasting and decision-support systems
  • High-performance numerical modeling
